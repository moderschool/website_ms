1. Introduction and Scope

Modern School, Kota ("The School," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy and security of the personal data of its students, prospective students, parents/guardians, faculty, staff, and website visitors (collectively referred to as "Data Principals").

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, process, share, and protect your Personal Data, particularly in relation to the school's website, modernschool.ac.in, and all related online and offline services (the "Services"). By accessing or using our Services, you consent to the data practices described in this policy.

2. Personal Data Collected

We collect various types of Personal Data for academic, administrative, and communication purposes. This data may be collected directly from you, your child (the student), or automatically through your use of the website.

Category of Data Examples of Data Collected Purpose of Collection
Identity Data Name, Date of Birth, Gender, Photograph, Aadhaar Number, Admission Number, Passport details (for trips/exchanges). Admission, Enrolment, Identity Verification, Record-keeping, Compliance.
Contact Data Residential Address, Email Address (Parent/Student), Phone Number (Parent/Student), Emergency Contact Details. Communication, Emergency Notifications, Fee Collection.
Academic/Educational Data Marks, Grades, Attendance records, Examination results, Assignments, Learning profiles, Academic progression. Educational delivery, Monitoring, Assessment, Issuance of Certificates/Transcripts.
Sensitive Personal Data Financial Information (bank/card details for fee payments), Medical records, Health history, Biometric information (e.g., for attendance/access, if used). Fee processing, Health and safety management, Compliance. Requires explicit consent.
Digital/Technical Data IP address, Browser type, Operating System, Device information, Pages visited, Time spent on the website. Website functionality, Security, Analytics, and Improvement of user experience.
Marketing/Communication Data Preferences for receiving newsletters, promotional materials, or updates. Dissemination of school news and events.

3. How We Collect Personal Data

  • Directly from You: When you complete an admission inquiry form, an online application, registration forms, fee payment forms, surveys, or communicate with us via email or phone.
  • From the Student: Through academic submissions, attendance marking, and participation in school activities.
  • Automatically: Through the school website using cookies, web analytics tools, and server logs when you browse modernschool.ac.in.
  • Offline: Through physical forms, documents, and records maintained by the School Administration.

4. Purpose and Lawful Basis for Processing

We process your Personal Data only for the specific, legitimate, and necessary purposes for which it was collected. The legal bases for our processing include:

Lawful Basis Purpose of Processing
Contractual Obligation To perform the contract of educational services (e.g., managing admission, academics, and school administration).
Legitimate Interest To improve and secure our Services, manage school operations, prevent fraud, and conduct general administration.
Legal Compliance To comply with legal and regulatory obligations, including those mandated by the CBSE/ICSE Board, State Education Departments, and Indian laws.
Consent (Explicit) For processing Sensitive Personal Data, sharing your data for promotional/marketing purposes, or for any purpose not directly related to the educational contract.

5. Special Note on Children's Data (Minors under 18)

As an educational institution, we necessarily process the Personal Data of children.

  • In compliance with the DPDP Act, we will process children's data only after obtaining Verifiable Consent from the parent or lawful guardian.
  • We only process the data strictly necessary for the child's education and school-related activities.
  • We do not track children, monitor their behaviour, or use their Personal Data for targeted advertising.
  • Parents/Guardians have the right to access, review, and request changes or deletion of their child's Personal Data.

6. Disclosure and Sharing of Personal Data

The School is committed to data minimisation and sharing data only when necessary and secure. We may share Personal Data with the following parties:

  • Internal Staff: Teachers, administrative staff, and relevant personnel who have a legitimate need-to-know to perform their duties.
  • Regulatory/Affiliating Bodies: Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE), State Education Department, or other government/statutory authorities as required by law.
  • Third-Party Service Providers (Data Processors): Payment Gateways, EdTech Platforms, Security/Transport Services, and IT Service Providers.
  • Legal Requirements: If we are required by law or a valid legal process (e.g., court order) to disclose information.

We do not sell or rent Personal Data to any third parties for marketing purposes.

7. Data Security and Retention

7.1 Security Measures

We implement reasonable security practices and procedures (including SSL technology, access controls, and authentication) to protect your Personal Data from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction, in line with applicable Indian laws.

7.2 Data Retention

We retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. Academic records will be retained for a longer duration for verification purposes. Once the retention period expires, we securely destroy or de-identify your Personal Data.

8. Your Rights as a Data Principal

You have the following rights regarding your Personal Data:

  • Right to Information/Access: Request a copy of the Personal Data we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction/Rectification: Request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to be Forgotten): Request the deletion of your Personal Data, subject to legal and regulatory retention periods.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw your consent at any time where we relied on consent to process your data.
  • Right to Grievance Redressal: Contact our Grievance Officer regarding any concerns.

9. Website Analytics and Cookies

Our website uses cookies to collect standard internet log information and visitor behaviour in an aggregated, non-personally identifiable form to analyse traffic and improve functionality. You can set your browser to refuse cookies, though some parts of the website may not function correctly if you do so.

10. Links to Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to external sites (e.g., payment gateways) that are not operated by us. We are not responsible for the content or privacy practices of these third-party sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies.

11. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to update this Privacy Policy at any time. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the new policy on this page and updating the "Effective Date."
